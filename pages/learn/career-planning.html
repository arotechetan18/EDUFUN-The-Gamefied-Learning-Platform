Career Planning

Career planning means understanding your interests, building useful skills, and moving step by step toward a long-term goal. A good plan is flexible and realistic.

Know Yourself

Think about what subjects you enjoy, what work you are curious about, and what strengths you already have.

Build Skills

Choose one practical skill to improve every few months. Communication, digital skills, and problem solving are valuable in many careers.

Track Progress

Use projects, certificates, notes, and score history to see how you are improving over time.

Simple Career Roadmap

  • Choose a goal based on interest and ability.
  • Find out what knowledge and skills that goal needs.
  • Make a weekly learning plan.
  • Practice, review, and improve using feedback.

Professional Habits For Learners

  • Be consistent even when progress feels slow.
  • Finish small tasks instead of starting too many.
  • Keep a portfolio of your work and achievements.
  • Ask questions and learn from mentors, teachers, and examples.
