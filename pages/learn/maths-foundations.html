Math Foundations

Math becomes easier when learners follow a process: understand the question, choose the correct method, solve carefully, and check the final answer.

Fractions

Fractions show parts of a whole. To compare fractions, make the denominators the same or convert them into decimals.

Ratios

A ratio compares two quantities. Always keep the units the same before simplifying.

Algebra

In algebra, letters stand for numbers. Solve equations by keeping both sides balanced.

Problem Solving Steps

  • Read the question slowly and underline the important values.
  • Decide whether the problem needs addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, or an equation.
  • Show the steps clearly so mistakes are easier to spot.
  • Check if the answer makes sense in real life.

Accuracy Habits

  • Write numbers neatly and line them up correctly.
  • Recheck signs such as plus, minus, multiplication, and division.
  • Estimate the answer first to see if the final result is reasonable.
  • Practice tables and mental math for faster solving.

Revision Tip

Solve a small set of mixed questions every day. Daily practice is more powerful than solving many questions once a week.
