Science Basics

Science helps us understand how the world works. Good science learning starts with observation, asking questions, testing ideas, and using evidence before making conclusions.

Matter

Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Solids, liquids, and gases are the common states of matter.

Force

A force is a push or a pull. Forces can change the speed, shape, or direction of an object.

Energy

Energy is the ability to do work. It can change form, such as heat to light or electrical energy to sound.

Scientific Method

  • Observe something carefully.
  • Ask a question about what you noticed.
  • Make a simple prediction.
  • Test the prediction with an experiment.
  • Record the result and explain what it means.

Everyday Science Examples

  • Ice melts because heat changes a solid into a liquid.
  • A ball rolls farther on a smooth surface because there is less friction.
  • Plants need sunlight, water, air, and nutrients to grow well.
  • Magnets attract some metals and show invisible force in action.

Science Learning Tip

When learning science, connect the topic to a real example. Understanding improves faster when the idea is linked to something you can see or do.
