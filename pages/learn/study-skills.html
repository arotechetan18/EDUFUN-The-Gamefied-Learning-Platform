Study Skills

Strong study habits help learners remember more, feel less stressed, and perform better in tests. Use these notes to build a routine that is calm, practical, and easy to follow every day.

Focus Rule

Study in short blocks of 25 to 35 minutes, then take a small break. Short sessions improve concentration better than long distracted sessions.

Recall Rule

Close the book and say the answer from memory. If you can explain it without looking, you are learning it properly.

Revision Rule

Review on the same day, then again after a few days. Spaced revision helps facts stay in long-term memory.

How To Study Better

1. Read the topic once for understanding.
2. Write three key points in your own words.
3. Practice one quiz, worksheet, or example question.
4. Check mistakes and note what needs revision.

Exam Preparation Notes

  • Start with topics you find difficult, not only the easy ones.
  • Use a checklist so you can see what is complete and what is pending.
  • Practice writing answers within a time limit.
  • Sleep well before an exam because tired revision is less effective.

Common Mistakes To Avoid

  • Reading without testing yourself.
  • Studying many subjects at once without a plan.
  • Skipping revision after learning a new topic.
  • Ignoring mistakes instead of understanding them.
