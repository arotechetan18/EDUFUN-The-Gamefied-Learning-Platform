EduFun Study Guides

These notes are written to help learners revise faster, understand key ideas clearly, and build confidence before quizzes. Each guide focuses on the essentials first, then gives a practical way to use the topic in real learning.

How To Study Better

Strong learning comes from short focused sessions, active recall, and revision over time instead of one long reading session.

  • Read a topic once for understanding.
  • Close the page and explain it in your own words.
  • Practice with 5-10 quick questions.
  • Review the same topic again after one day and one week.
Quick tip: If you cannot explain a topic simply, revise the basics again before moving ahead.

Math Problem Solving

Most maths mistakes happen because students rush into calculation before understanding the question.

  • Underline what is given.
  • Identify what must be found.
  • Choose the formula or concept first.
  • Check the unit and sign in the final answer.
Remember: Accuracy grows when you solve slowly first and then increase speed later.

Science Revision Method

Science becomes easier when you revise with cause and effect. Ask what happens, why it happens, and where it is used.

  • Write one-line definitions.
  • List important formulas or laws.
  • Draw the process in a simple diagram.
  • Connect the idea to daily life examples.
Best practice: Turn every science chapter into definitions, diagrams, and examples.

English And Communication

Language skill improves through reading, vocabulary building, and short writing practice every day.

  • Learn 3-5 new words daily.
  • Read one short paragraph aloud.
  • Write a 5-line summary in simple English.
  • Notice sentence structure and punctuation.
Simple rule: Clear communication is better than difficult words used incorrectly.

Coding Roadmap Basics

Programming is easier when learners build from logic to syntax and from syntax to projects.

  • Start with variables, conditions, and loops.
  • Practice small problems daily.
  • Learn functions and reusable logic.
  • Build tiny projects such as calculators or quiz apps.
Focus on writing and testing code yourself. Watching tutorials alone is not enough.

Exam Preparation Workflow

Good exam preparation is organized, measurable, and realistic. A strong plan always includes revision and testing.

  • Break subjects into small topics.
  • Set daily goals with one revision target.
  • Take short self-tests after every study block.
  • Keep the last phase for weak areas only.
A clear plan reduces stress because it shows what is finished and what still needs attention.
